Serial Tap Wiring

The CTS Gen6 outputs its serial data on the 1/4" headphone jack. Tap it passively with a Y-cable — one side goes back to the CTS display, the other to the DB9 connector. 

1/4" Y-cable — center conductor (tip) → DB9 pin 2  (RX)
1/4" Y-cable — shield (sleeve)        → DB9 pin 5  (GND)
DB9 PinSignalWire
Pin 2RX (receive data)Center conductor (tip) of 1/4" cable
Pin 5GND (signal ground)Shield (sleeve) of 1/4" cable
Protocol: 9600 baud, 8-E-1 (8 data bits, even parity, 1 stop bit)
Network

All pool-deck devices communicate over a dedicated wired network on eth0. Pi #1 also connects to home WiFi (wlan0) for internet access and remote management.

DeviceIP addressRole
Pi #110.10.10.10Serial decoder + Flask server + admin UI
Pi #2DHCPChromium kiosk — displays scoreboard on TV
LaptopDHCP or staticAdmin access via browser to 10.10.10.10:5000/settings
Firewall: Pi #1 blocks all incoming connections over WiFi. SSH and VNC are only reachable via eth0. Connect your laptop by ethernet to reach the admin UI or terminal at the pool.
